Biot Savart Law

For a circular current loop, the magnetic field of a current element dI can be found first by looking at the infinitesimal magnetic field element 'dB' generated due to the current: $$ \color{#E47F1A}{d\mathbf{B}} = \frac{{\mu}_oI}{4\pi} \times \frac{\color{#1A40B1}{d\mathbf{l}} \times \color{#2E9849}{\mathbf{r}}}{r^3}$$ To calculate the total magnetic field at the point P we must integrate the infinitesimal magnetic field element across the entire loop: $$\color{#D81C1C}{\mathbf{B}} = \frac{{\mu}_oI}{4\pi} \int_{C}\frac{d\mathbf{l} \times \mathbf{r}}{r^2} $$
